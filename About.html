Second, I'd like to clarify, my name is Cole Lamers (Kohl Lay-merz). For a fairly anglicanized name, you'd be amazed at how many people get it wrong. The last name most people say "Lammers" like "Lah-mers" or "Lamb-mers." I think it's just cause people don't want to say the word "lame." But it is somewhat fitting since apparently the reason we have the last name is cause one of my Dutch ancestors just decided to give his children the name. His name was "Lambertus", and my ascestor being unoriginal, just dropped the BTU. He removed the only shred of energy from our name. Along with, I've also been called Cole, Cody, Paul, Cool, Vance, Logan (Oh wait, sorry I'm now complaining about being a middle child, my bad). As far as I'm concerned, my name is actually (spelling) C-O-L-E-L-A-M-E-R-S. At this stage, I'm really thinking about getting a name change to John Smith.

But first, I'd like to apologize for the horrible German, it's a hard language and even some italki teachers told me to practice more. But I blame them for having such a complicated language. Even Mark Twain criticized it, so I feel somewhat vindicated by my efforts. Talking like an idiot but being understood is 10x better than not being able to speak it at all. So take that Germans.

Open mic and one man band

I run faster than a forest, but not faster THAN Forest

I like to run in my spare time though. Keeps me healthy and reminds me to check my pulse from time to time. It is my rock, along with my exercise bike if I need to give my knees a break. I never understood runners high until after a couple years of running. Started with a mile, bumped it to a 5k, then 10k for a while, then back to 5k. On occassion I'll run even when it's in the negatives if the wind isn't bad. That's one thing you'll learn when running. The wind is afwul. And somehow out of the entire world, I live in one of the windiest regions. If I was world dictator, my first edict would be to ban the wind, because frankly I'm sick of 40mph+ gusts beating down my chest when I'm just trying to move forward. Vote for me, 20XX.
