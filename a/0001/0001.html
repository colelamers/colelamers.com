Howdy!

First, I'd like to clarify, my name is Cole Lamers, pronounced (Kohl Lay-merz). For a fairly anglicanized name, you'd be amazed at how many people get it wrong. The last name most people say "Lah-mers" or "Lamb-mers." I think it's just cause people don't want to say the word "lame." Apparently the reason we have the last name is cause one of my Dutch ancestors just decided to give his children the name. His name was "Lambertus", and my ancestor being unoriginal, just dropped the BTU. He removed the only shred of energy from our name.

To sort of give a basic rundown of my heritage of what I've found, I'm basically as Germanic/Frankish as they come. Since none of you have the evidence to prove me wrong, I'm related to Charlemagne. Like all good pretenders, I will stake my own claim on faulty evidence and irrational connections.

  • Basically my Grandmother on my mothers side is primarily Dutch/Low country.
  • My Grandfather on my fathers side is pretty much 100% Dutch.
  • My Grandfather on my mothers side is Pomeranian/Palatinate German with some Dutch, Belgian, and Luxembourger to boot. For those of you who aren't aware, these people (besides the Pomeranians) are basically within a 50 mile radius.
  • Lastly, my Grandmother on my fathers side is basically the "mutt" of Europeans. She is: Polish, French (like every kind including Canadian, excluding Parisian), German (mostly ex-Prussian German...so basically Polish), and a dash of English.

I am not a big traveler, but I have been known to dabble in the middle class art of throwing money away to a state that I don't live in. I never really understood the purpose of it, but it's neat to do as it gives you a different perspective of life. One thought I've had on the process is that it's essentially a startup where you throw money at it to stave boredom away. Then when you get to the airport, you really start justifying reasons to spend money because you're so phenomenally bored, waiting to get on the plane while a family of 16 who has never played Tetris figures out how to put their carry ons in the luggage compartment for their 1 day stay in Whocaresvilles.

I run faster than a forest, but not faster THAN Forest

Running has been my "Stand Rock" of the very stoney Wisconsin Dells. Keeps me healthy and reminds me to check my pulse from time to time. That and along with my exercise bike if I need to give my knees a break. I never understood runners high until after a couple years of running. Started with a mile, bumped it to a 5k, then 10k for a while, then back to 5k. On occasion I'll run even when it's in the negatives if the wind isn't bad. That's one thing you'll learn when running. The wind is awful. And somehow out of the entire world, I live in one of the windiest regions. If I was world dictator, my first edict would be to ban the wind, because frankly I'm sick of 40mph+ gusts beating down my chest when I'm just trying to move forward. Vote for me, 20XX.

As of right now, that's basically me in a nutshell. Hope to keep doing this more though in the long term and make more updates as I go.
